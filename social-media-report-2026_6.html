Monthly analysis & recommendations — Ninel Anderson · LinkedIn · March 2026
What's working
Original CEO voice is the clear winner. 2 original posts averaged 3.28% engagement rate vs 2.24% for DS reshares (+46% delta). The LinkedIn vs reality meme (Mar 17) led the month: 70 eng, 1,995 impr, 3.51% rate. Self-deprecating founder humour is Ninel's highest-ROI format.
Strong IPs amplify reshares. The DS Risk of Rain 2 case study (Mar 12) hit 2,529 impressions — top reach of the month. Recognised game IPs pull audience beyond Ninel's core followers into gaming communities.
What needs improving
57% of posts are DS reshares, only 1 has personal commentary. The Ryan Lastimosa post (Mar 7, "Let's go 🚀") with added text got 44 eng vs 15 for the Tracer reshare with no text. Each reshare without a personal line is a missed opportunity to distinguish Ninel's voice from the DS company page.
Volume down and content thin. 7 posts in March vs 9 in Feb. Only 2 original posts (28%) out of 7. At this ratio, Ninel's personal brand increasingly looks like a DS satellite account — not a founder voice with an independent audience.
April recommendations
Always add personal commentary to reshares. Even 1–2 sentences ("This is the deal we're most proud of this quarter because…") raises eng rate from 2.24% to the 3.5%+ territory of original posts. Make it a rule: no blank reshares.
Double down on original founder formats. The meme format works — replicate it. Beyond memes: "what I got wrong about X", "3 lessons from 10 years building studios", GDC recap in Ninel's voice. Target 4+ original posts in April.
Monthly impressions benchmark: 25,000+. March hit 20,026 across 7 posts. Adding 2 more strong originals would likely push past 25K. Use post-05 (ROR2) as the reshare template: known IP + case study depth.

Monthly analysis & recommendations — Fusion by Devoted · LinkedIn · March 2026
✅ What's working
Visual art posts drive the highest reach. Alan P. (747 impr) and Gabriel F. (393 impr) outperformed every #features post. Scroll-stopping artwork with a distinct style is the strongest organic signal Fusion has — the audience responds to seeing something beautiful before they read a word.
Process carousels convert attention into engagement. The NDA → KYC → Compliance document post hit 6.61% eng.% and 4.2% CTR — the best full-data result of the month. Art directors want a hiring flow they can trust; step-by-step visuals give them that clearly.
Data hooks open strong. The 50%+ freelancer rate pressure post and the 80% Asia statistic both landed above average for their rubric. Numbers in the first line stop the scroll for an audience that lives and breathes production budgets.
⚠️ What needs improving
Content is too narrow — 3 rubrics out of 6 used. #meme, #USP, and #collection_highlight had zero posts in March. The weekly diversity rule exists specifically to prevent this. Without variety, the feed feels like a portfolio catalogue, not a brand with a point of view.
#features posts are over-repeating the KYC/compliance topic. 3 out of 5 #features posts touched KYC, NDA, or fraud protection. The audience has seen the message — repeating it without a new angle or client proof point generates diminishing returns.
Most artist spotlights lack a strong CTA. Posts for Santiago B., Eva M., and Charlotte B. averaged under 150 impressions. Without a "browse this style on Fusion" link or a direct instruction to an art director, they drive awareness without any conversion signal.
💡 April recommendations
Add the missing rubrics — at minimum 1 meme and 1 #collection_highlight. A Fusion meme targeting art directors ("when the brief says 'simple character art'") would be the highest-shareability post of the month. A curated collection ("5 stylized environment artists on Fusion right now") drives platform browse intent.
Pair every artist spotlight with a direct CTA. "Browse [style] on Fusion →" with a link to the search category. This turns a passive awareness post into a measurable conversion touchpoint. Even without click analytics, the habit builds the right post structure.
Retire KYC/compliance as a standalone topic for April. Fold it into a client proof post instead — "Studio X hired 3 artists through Fusion in 72 hours, fully vetted" communicates the same trust signal with a real story behind it. More credible, more shareable.
